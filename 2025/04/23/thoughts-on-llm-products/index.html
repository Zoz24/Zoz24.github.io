Bits of thoughts on LLM based products

In this post, I share my evolving perspective on the opportunities and pitfalls of building products powered by large language models (LLMs).

The Current Landscape

LLMs are rapidly improving—what felt cutting-edge six months ago may already be obsolete today. Startups that base their entire value proposition on a specific LLM risk being outpaced when a more powerful, general-purpose model emerges. Conversely, great ideas can stall because current LLM capabilities aren’t quite there yet.

Core Principles for LLM-Based Products (Personal thoughts)

When it comes to building something that relies on LLM, I think there are few points that is worthy of attention.

  1. Use LLMs as Enablers, Not the Core Engine
  2. Design for Continuous Improvement
  3. Leverage Unique Domain Expertise

Successful products solve real problems first, then use AI to enhance the solution. If the underlying idea lacks substance, adding an LLM won’t magically make it compelling. Prioritize genuine user needs—even if that means building something you personally rely on—before layering on AI.

Since LLMs will only get better, architect your product so that each model upgrade seamlessly enhances the user experience. Rather than hard-coding model-specific logic, build a flexible pipeline where you can swap in new models or tweak prompts without rewriting your entire codebase. In short: ride the wave, don’t fight it.

Your strongest moat is specialized knowledge that general-purpose LLMs lack. If you’re deeply familiar with a niche—whether it’s medical billing, supply-chain logistics, or vintage auto restoration—you can craft prompts, data curation, and workflow integrations that outperform out-of-the-box solutions. Focus on tailored experiences rather than broad-stroke features.

Additional Insights

  • Personal Taste Matters: Develop your own instincts about what makes a delightful experience. User empathy and design instincts often differentiate products more than raw LLM power. Don’t think like others but think like yourself, I don’t mean to not listen to other opinions and advice but if you know a specific domain very well and you know exactly how LLM can be put into it for maximal gains, that’s your biggest edge over other companies. Your product won’t be killed because it is not a generic purpose tool like reading PDF which can easily get reproduced by giants like Google or OpenAI. You should instead focus on something you know so well or you want to know so well, then tackle it, and because of that distinction, it’ll produce its distinctive value.
